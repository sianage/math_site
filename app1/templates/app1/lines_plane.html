This problem is similar to the Tower of Hanoi problem in that it can be reduced to a series of smaller problems whose solutions are instances of the larger problem. How many slices of pizza can a person obtain by making n straight cuts? More formally, what is the maximum number Ln of regions defined by n lines in the plane?

pics

The pattern that arises is the following:
L0 = 1 L1 = 2 L2 = 4 L3 = 7
The pattern can be explained as the nth line increases the number of regions by k if and only if it splits k of the old regions, and it splits k of the old regions, and it splits k old regions if and only if it hits the previous lines in k-1 different places. Two lines can intersect in at most one point, so the new line drawn can intersect at most n-1 lines in at most n-1 points. This yields the equation Ln <= Ln-1 + n, for n > 0.
