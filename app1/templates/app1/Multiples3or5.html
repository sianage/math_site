The Tower of Hanoi

The Tower of Hanoi is a puzzle in which there are a number of disks and three pegs. You need to move the disks from one peg to another, and you aren't allowed to ever place a larger disk on top of a smaller disk.

This is a problem which can be broken down into multiple instances of the same problem. Take, for example, the scenario where you have 2 disks. First you move the top disk to an empty peg, then you move the larger disk to the other peg, then you move the smaller disk on top of the larger disk again. Now, if you take the scenario where you have 3 disks, first you move the top (smallest) disk to a different peg, then you move the middle disk to another peg, then you move the smallest disk on top of the middle disk. Finally, you move the bottom (largest) disk to the empty peg. This is where the problem repeats itself. The next three steps are exactly the same as the previous example of 2 disks. Since this pattern repeats itself no matter how many disks you have, it is a perfect candidate to be solved by recursion.

The general solution to this problem for n disks is as follows:

  1. transfer every one but the largest one to a different peg (Tn-1 moves)
  2. move the largest one to the empty peg (1 move)
  3. transfer the other disks onto the largest (Tn-1 moves)

This yields a final formula of Tn = 2Tn-1 + 1
